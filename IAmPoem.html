I Am Poem





I AM Shicheng Lai



I am an introvert with an introspective mind. They say that I am timid or indifferent, but I simply want to reserve the wonders of my days with the significant individuals in my life.

I wonder if staying in one's confident zone is a sign of timidity or courage. Choosing to enjoy a peculiar, narrow range of activities and have a small group of friends is perhaps more challenging in a society that keeps on emphasizing the superiority of being "social", "trendy", "popular"...

I hear the lamenting notes of cello mixing together with the crisp sound of piano. This duo of instruments that perhaps only I will find the time to appreciate.

I see myself standing ten feet away from the center of that imaginary circle. Far enough that I don’t have to be an active participant in the heated argument, yet close enough that I am aware of the situations around me.

I want to be able to deliver my thoughts clearly under any condition, whether during a MUN debate or a random conversation with my parents.

I am an introvert with an introspective mind. They say that I am timid or indifferent, but I simply want to reserve the wonders of my days with the significant individuals in my life.



I pretend to be optimistic because my high school chemistry teacher emphasizes “fake it till you make it” and because I superstitiously believe that it will bring me good luck.

I feel anger, joy, heartbreak, but they don’t see. They say I seem distant.

I touch the watch my grandma bought me, and as if psychometry does exist, I can sense her love - strong, affirming love that allows me to see myself as a wonderful human being.

I worry, paradoxically, that I lose too much hair from entangling myself in a wide variety of problems. They can be as trivial as “which song should I use for my alarm”, or as philosophical as “why is there societal expectation?”

I cry when I falter or have bad days, but my logics can soon find the "north star" when the blues flush away with my tears.

I am an introvert with an introspective mind. They say that I am timid or indifferent, but I simply want to reserve the wonders of my days with the significant individuals in my life.

I understand that I cannot suddenly become Margaret Atwood, Chopin, or Dale Carnegie. Though I still aim to shape my voice through their inspiration.

I say to myself that I appreciate being who I am. By saying this, I become more aware of my own strengths. Even though I can’t express myself well enough for others to see the value in me, I can still value myself. I don’t need to rely on others’ opinions, and this makes me confident.

I dream small dreams for today, hoping that I will not slip when I walk across the icy road. But, I dream big dreams for the future, expecting to obtain economic freedom in my thirties.

I try to share my true self but the stilted language is holding me back and protecting my vulnerable self from the sharp swords of your judgements.

I hope that telepathy is real, so there will be no miscommunication through words or facial expressions, so people can grasp an precise understanding of who I am. I hope they can realize that under my quietness lays positivity and confidence rather than apathy.

I am an introvert with an introspective mind. They say that I am timid or indifferent, but I simply want to reserve the wonders of my days with the significant individuals in my life.
